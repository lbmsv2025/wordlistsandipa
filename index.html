What are Minimal Pairs?

Minimal pairs are two words that sound almost the same, but have one different sound.

Because of that small sound difference, the meaning of the word also changes. Practicing minimal pairs helps learners hear and pronounce English sounds more clearly.

For example, ship and sheep are minimal pairs. They are different because of the vowel sound: /…™/ in ship and /iЋР/ in sheep.
